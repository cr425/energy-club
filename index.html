About Us

The Johnson Energy Club is comprised of highly motivated students who are passionate about making an impact in the energy industry. The interests of the club span both conventional and renewable energy, and are spread across various functional areas including but not limited to: sales & trading, managerial finance, consulting, investment banking, marketing, and entrepreneurship.

Mission

To educate the student body and club members about the latest trends in the energy industry, host energy-focused events, build strong relationships with energy companies, and ultimately place our members in leadership positions at top-tier energy companies.

Events

The Energy Club presents students with a variety of opportunities year round to engage in the energy world and its myriad possibilities. Besides exposing students to the field through speakers at the height of their expertise, the Energy Club gives its members hands-on experience participating in events like the annual Renewable Energy Case Competition at the Ross School of Business. Members tap into the club's vast network of energy career connections through campus networking events and through direct, on-site engagement with energy companies. A list of the Energy Club's upcoming events and recurring events can be found below.

Annual Events

Renewable Energy Case Competition

Each year, the Energy Club sends members to Michigan's prestigious Ross School of Business to compete in the Renewable Energy Case Competition, which brings together top MBA students from all over the U.S. Competitors solve problems relevant and pressing in the energy industry today and gain valuable and practical insight for future careers.

Johnson Energy Connection

The Johnson Energy Connection, co-sponsored by the Energy Club, takes advantage of Cornell's extensive alumni network to provide an interface between students and alumni working in the energy industry. Experts in sustainable energy, conventional energy, energy economics, and everything in between, coming from companies like Chevron, GE, and EnerNOC, gather in Sage Hall to share their experiences and meet with students.

Trek to GE

Energy Club members embark on a day-trip to Schenectady, New York, home of the General Electric Renewable Energy World Headquarters, where they gain direct access to information and meetings with employees of the worldwide energy giant.
